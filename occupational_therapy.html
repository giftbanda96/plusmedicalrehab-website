OCCUPATIONAL THERAPY

These service aim at enhancing a person’s ability to do basic self-care, useful work and leisure activities which have become hard to do as a result of an injury or a disorder. These activities include eating, dressing, bathing, grooming, and toileting, preparing meals, managing finances, shopping, and driving, sporting activities etc.

This therapy focuses mainly on the ability to create and execute a plan and the ability to want to do the activity and to persevere until it is completed in the optimum possible way.

Conditions that require occupation therapy include; neurological conditions (e.g. stroke, spinal cord injuries, multiple sclerosis, Parkinson disease, traumatic brain injuries etc.), musculoskeletal conditions (e.g. Low and upper back pain, Disc herniation, Arthritis (Gout, Rheumatoid, Osteoarthritis and septic), fractures, neck pain, Joint pain), burns and pediatric conditions (e.g. Cerebral palsy, Autisms, Hydrocephalus, Developments delay, Flat foot, autism, spinal bifida, attention deficit hyperactive disorder, learning and intellectual disabilities, Arthrogryposis, club foot, erb’s palsy, down’s syndrome) etc
