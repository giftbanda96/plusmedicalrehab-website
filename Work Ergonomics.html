WORK ERGONOMICS

Health education and technical guidance on work-related hazards are provided to employees and management in organizations. Work hazards are physical factors that can harm individuals, and ergonomics focuses on adjusting the work environment, using ergonomically sound equipment, and modifying tasks to improve efficiency and reduce discomfort or injury risk. This includes avoiding awkward postures, repetitive tasks, forceful exertions, and lifting heavy objects.

The effects of awkward postures includes placing the muscles and joints out of balance, making tasks more physically demanding, and adding stress to the body. This increase chances of injury especially when a joint (s) is not in the neutral position.

We come up with programs that promote body and joints awareness in relation to space when doing work related activity(s) to avoid injuries.

Overhead activities, squatting or kneeling, repetitive bending of the neck more than 45° without support, or frequent posture changes for prolonged periods Hands, elbows, shoulders, neck, back hips, knees, and ankles can lead to stress injuries over time because they do not keep the joints in a neutral position.

We assess individual workstation and come up with individualised intervetions to avoid injuries caused by repetitive tasks

Forceful exertions in manual tasks like mechanics, plumbing, shop custodian, and landscape work can lead to injuries. Common tasks that cause ergonomic injuries include mopping, sweeping, dusting, cleaning, scrubbing, vacuuming, using power equipment and tools, typing, using hand tools, and overhead work.

To prevent injuries, choose tools with smooth, non-slippery, padded handles, avoid gripping or pinching with wrists in awkward positions, take frequent breaks, observe neutral positions, alternate activities frequently, use two hands to lift objects, slide or push and pull objects instead of lifting, avoid jerky movements when lifting objects, use power devices when available, keep reaching to a minimum, use stepstools or ladders when necessary, avoid using body parts as impact devices, and use protective equipment when possible. Maintaining neutral body positioning is crucial for reducing stress and strain on muscles, tendons, and skeletal system, and reducing the risk of developing musculoskeletal disorders.
